death penalty
/ˈdeTH ˌpenəltē/
noun
A cruel and unusual punishment imposed on those over the age of 18. Definition by Four to Five Podcast crew

We at Four to Five create podcasts that address the death penalty – particularly the cut off for the imposition of the death penalty being a young age of 18 years. In light of scientific evidence of adolescent brain development, lack of national consensus, case precedence, and possibility for juvenile rehabilitation, this age should be expanded to protect those 25 years old. Such an expansion will allow for the defendant’s cases and mental culpability to be individually considered.

-Pleasant Ballenger, Yash Banga, Jessica Hoffman, Lilli Beaty, and Liyah Smalls
